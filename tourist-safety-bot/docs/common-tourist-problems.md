# Common Tourist Problems in Thailand

Taxi or transport overcharging:
- Incident type: scam or transport.
- Severity: medium if money dispute, threat, or refusal to return belongings is involved.
- First steps: keep receipt, route screenshot, fare quote, vehicle photo, license plate, driver app profile, and location.
- Ask for pickup/dropoff location, approximate fare paid/quoted, time, and evidence.

Lost passport:
- Incident type: lost_item.
- Severity: medium if passport is lost or stolen.
- First steps: check recent locations, contact venue/hotel/transport lost and found, prepare passport copy/photo if available, contact embassy or consulate.
- Ask for nationality, last known location, time, and contact method.

Theft, robbery, assault, threats:
- Incident type: crime.
- Severity: high.
- First steps: move to safety, avoid confronting suspects, contact local police or security, preserve evidence.
- Ask for current safe location, injuries, suspect description, time, and contact method.

Medical emergency or accident:
- Incident type: medical_emergency or accident.
- Severity: high if injured, bleeding, unconscious, severe pain, or traffic crash.
- First steps: call 1669 or local emergency services, ask nearby staff/security for help, avoid moving seriously injured people unless in immediate danger.
- Ask for location, injury details, number of people affected, and contact method.

Visa, immigration, or detention concerns:
- Incident type: immigration.
- Severity: medium or high depending on urgency.
- First steps: stay calm, keep passport and documents ready, ask for interpreter or official explanation, contact embassy if needed.
- Ask for location, nationality, document status, and what official agency is involved.
